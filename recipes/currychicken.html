Jamaican Curry Chicken Recipe

Jamaican curry chicken was a regular staple growing up, and now it is for my own family.

Now you may be saying to yourself...wait, a curry is a curry. Not so fast. There is a slight difference between Jamaican curry and Indian curry. Jamaican curry has more tumeric, allspice (pimento), scotch bonnet peppers, and nutmeg. It is not only more yellow in colour, it also has a distinct difference in flavor.

Ingredients

  • 4 to 5 lbs chicken
  • Lemon juice or vinegar, for rinsing the chicken
  • 1 Tbsp all purpose seasoning
  • 1 Tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 Tsp black pepper
  • 6 Tbsp Jamaican curry powder
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/4 red bell pepper
  • 1/4 green bell pepper
  • 1 onion chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 2 to 4 scotch bonnet or habenaro peppers, seeds removed
  • 2 potatoes, chopped
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Directions

  1. Wash chicken with lemon juice and water. Then rinse with water and pat chicken dry.
  2. Chop chicken into small pieces and place in a bowl.
  3. Sprinkle all seasonings over the chicken (hold back 2 Tbsp of curry powder). Using food safe gloves, rub seasonings over the chicken until fully covered.
  4. Cover bowl of seasoned chicken and place in refrigerater for at least 1 hour.
  5. When ready to cook, allow chicken to sit for 20 minutes
  6. Heat vegetable oil on medium heat in a sauce pan. Add 2 Tbsp of Jamaican curry powder stir for 30 to 40 seconds
  7. Then add onions, minced garlic, and bell peppers and saute them until soft.
  8. Add chicken and mix in
  9. Slowly add the room temperature water
  10. Add potatoes and carrots
  11. Once pot comes to a boil, reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and allow to simmer for 1 hour.
  12. Serve with white rice or rice and peas.
