Jamaican Oven Roasted Jerk Pork

Oven Roasted Jamaican Jerk Pork

Traditionally, this dish is street food in Jamaica. Jerk pork is juicy and flavorful. Typically paired with rice and peas or Festivals (Jamaican fried corned bread).

Now perfection takes time. The longer you leave the pork to marinade, the more flavorful it will be. Whether you grill, smoke, or roast it in the oven, 'low and slow' is the only way to make it juicy and tender.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup ground allspice berries
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 4 to 6 Scotch Bonnet peppers, seeds removed
  • 1 Tbsp ground dried Thyme, or 3 Tbsp fresh Thyme leaves
  • 2 stalks Green Onion
  • 1 Tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 Tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2 Tsp kosher salt
  • 1/2 Tsp black pepper
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce

Roast

  • 6 to 9 lbs bone-in picnic pork shoulder or pork butt

Directions

  1. Combine all ingredients in a food processor and blend until smooth.
  2. With a sharp knife, score the fat of the roast using a diamond pattern.
  3. Using food safe gloves, massage the jerk sauce over the entire pork shoulder until it is covered. Any left over jerk sauce can be refrigerated.
  4. Place the pork into a large zip-loc bag or covered bowl and refrigerate it for 24 to 48 hours.
  5. When ready to cook, let the pork sit at room temperature for at least 1 hour and preheat the oven to 450 F.
  6. Place the pork on a roasting rack in a roasting pan.
  7. Roast uncovered for 30 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 325 F.
  8. Roast for an additional 3 to 4 hours at this temperature. Insert an instant-read thermometer the thickest part of the roast. The roast is done when the at a reading of 180 to 190 F.
  9. Remove from the oven and let it rest for 30 minutes before carving.
  10. Serve with rice and peas.
